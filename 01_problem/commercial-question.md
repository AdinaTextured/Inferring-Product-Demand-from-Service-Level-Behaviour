# Commercial Question
Can service-level data be used to determine whether a haircare product should be launched or repositioned in the UK market?

# Context
Haircare product decisions are driven by consumer trends, social media signals, and retail performance. 
However, these signals do not always reflect how hair is actually styled and maintained in professional settings.
This creates a risk that products are developed or launched based on perceived demand rather than structurally embedded demand.

# Hypothesis

Trend-driven signals may suggest demand for certain products (e.g. curl creams), 
but products that are consistently required within high-frequency services represent a more reliable indicator of commercial demand.

# This study focuses on:

Knotless braid services across UK-based stylists, selected due to:

high frequency across providers
consistent service structure
clear dependency on specific product categories

# Key Questions
Which braid services are most consistently offered?
What product categories are required to deliver these services?
Which product types show structural demand?
How does this compare to trend-driven product signals?
