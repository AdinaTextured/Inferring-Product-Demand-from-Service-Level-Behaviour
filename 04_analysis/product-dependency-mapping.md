# Product Dependency Mapping
This section maps knotless braid services to the product categories required to deliver them. The purpose is to identify which product types are structurally embedded within high-frequency services, and therefore represent reliable and repeatable demand. Unlike trend-driven approaches, which rely on consumer signals, this method focuses on the practical requirements of services that are consistently performed by stylists.

# Service Workflow
Knotless braid services follow a consistent and repeatable workflow across providers. The process typically involves preparing the hair, sectioning and installing braids using extensions, and finishing the style to ensure hold and neatness. In many cases, this is followed by ongoing maintenance, particularly for longer-term styles. Based on this workflow, four core product categories are required to deliver knotless braid services.

Braiding Hair: fundamental input and is required for every knotless braid service. The service cannot be performed without it, making this the most direct and unavoidable product dependency.

Hold Products: such as gel or edge control are used during installation to create clean parting, provide grip, and maintain structure throughout the braiding process. These products are used consistently across providers and form part of the standard installation workflow.

Finishing Products: most commonly mousse, are applied at the end of the service to set the style, reduce frizz, and improve overall appearance. This step is a consistent part of the service and is important for achieving the final look expected by clients.

Scalp Care: Scalp oils and similar products are used to maintain scalp health and finishing shine. These products may be applied during the service or recommended as part of aftercare.

# Structural Demand
Each of these product categories is required to deliver a service that appears consistently across all providers in the dataset. Because knotless braid services are a core, repeatable offering, the associated product categories represent: structurally embedded demand within the service ecosystem
This demand is driven by the requirements of the service itself, rather than by external trends or marketing signals. within knotless braid services, curl creams are not required as part of the core workflow. This highlights a key distinction; while curl creams may have consumer demand, they do not represent structurally embedded demand within this service segment.

#Commercial Implications

This mapping provides a reliable basis for product decision-making. A brand could use this approach to:

prioritise products that are required across high-frequency services
identify opportunities to reposition existing products within service workflows
reduce reliance on trend-driven product development
