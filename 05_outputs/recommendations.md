# Use Case 1: Evaluating a New Product Launch
A brand is considering launching a curl cream in the UK market.

Assessment

Trend signals suggest strong demand for curl definition products, particularly driven by social media and consumer-facing content. However, analysis of knotless braid services shows that curl creams are not required within the core workflow of this high-frequency service.

The service instead relies on:
braiding hair
hold products
finishing products (e.g. mousse)
scalp care products

These product categories are consistently used across providers and are structurally embedded within the service.

# Recommendation

A curl cream should not be prioritised as a primary product for this segment. While it may have consumer demand, it does not align with the operational requirements of a core, repeatable service. As a result, it represents trend-driven demand rather than structurally embedded demand, and carries higher risk if positioned as a core offering.

# Use Case 2: Identifying Product Opportunities
What product categories should be prioritised based on service-level demand?

Assessment

Knotless braid services appear consistently across all providers in the dataset and follow a repeatable workflow. This creates stable demand for a defined set of product categories:

braiding hair
hold products
finishing products
scalp care

Among these, finishing and scalp care products are particularly interesting, as they:

are used across multiple stages of the service lifecycle
are applied both during installation and ongoing maintenance
are more easily branded and differentiated than core inputs such as braiding hair

# Recommendation
Product development should prioritise categories that are directly embedded within the service workflow, particularly:
lightweight finishing products designed for braid styles
scalp care products tailored to protective styles
These categories offer a strong foundation for product development due to their consistent and repeatable use.

#Use Case 3: Repositioning Existing Products
How can existing products be repositioned for the afro hair service market?

Assessment

Many existing products may already align with the needs of knotless braid services but are not currently positioned within this context.
For example:
a generic mousse can function as a finishing product for braid styles
a general-purpose oil can be positioned as a scalp care product for protective styles
The gap is not necessarily in product formulation, but in contextual positioning and targeting.

#Recommendation
Brands should evaluate their existing product portfolio against service workflows and identify opportunities to reposition products in line with real usage patterns.
This may involve:

reframing product use cases around specific services
aligning messaging with stylist workflows
targeting professional or semi-professional use cases

This approach allows brands to enter new segments without developing entirely new products.

# Key Insight

The analysis highlights a fundamental distinction between two types of demand:

trend-driven demand, based on consumer signals
structurally embedded demand, based on service requirements

Knotless braid services demonstrate that structurally embedded demand can be identified through consistent service-level behaviour.

Products that support these services represent more reliable and repeatable commercial opportunities.

# Final Recommendation

Product strategy should prioritise products that are required to deliver high-frequency services, rather than products that are primarily driven by trends This approach provides a more stable and commercially grounded basis for decision-making.
